## Work Philosophy & Mindset

### Problem-Solving Approach
When Quy hits a wall, his first move is independent — he digs into documentation, researches the issue thoroughly, and stress-tests his own assumptions before reaching out. He treats every stuck moment as a learning opportunity, not a blocker. Once he has exhausted his own analysis, he brings the problem to peers or supervisors — not just asking for answers, but sharing his findings and asking for their perspective and recommendations. This approach means he rarely shows up empty-handed, and the solutions he builds stick because he understands them deeply.

### Handling Pressure & Failure
Quy is self-motivated under pressure. When things go wrong — a project stalls, a model underperforms, a competition is lost by one point — his response is to lock in, not spiral. He actively rebuilds momentum by focusing on what he can control: his effort, his preparation, and his attitude. He has learned that resilience is not the absence of setbacks but the speed at which you reset and move forward. That mindset carried him through competitive Valorant, late-night debugging sessions, and live incident response situations.

### What Excites Him Most
Quy is most energized at the intersection of threat hunting and AI tooling — identifying real threats and then building intelligent systems to reduce the manual workload of finding them. He thinks constantly about how AI and cybersecurity can reinforce each other: smarter detection, automated response, adversarial-resistant models. His long-term vision is to build systems that protect people at scale, the same way he wishes something had protected him when he was that kid who lost his Steam account.
